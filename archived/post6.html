Data scientist commenting on academia’s research

Accuracy performance and reflections from the industry

Small accuracy raise with big effort has limited commercial value

Since the WallStreetBet GME short squeeze incident, it sparks discussion over the untapped potential of social media for investment banks. Some researchers from the academia such as Dr. Anshul Gupta from Stanford University Department of Computer Science has explored whether the subreddit provides predictive ability on a stock’s price the following day. According to their reflection, their top models perform at roughly 52 and 58% accuracy, moderately improving upon random baseline of 50%. “That is fairly good for any accuracy raise, but in terms for commercial value that amount of increase has little commercial value, especially the model is not easy to build interpret.” Po said.

Prediction result of around 50% accuracy is not ideal

Testing and training data in researches should be from different timeframe

Similarly, Charlie Wang and Ben Luo from University of Illinois at Chicago Department of Computer Science published a paper titled “Predicting $GME Stock Price Movement Using Sentiment from Reddit r/wallstreetbets” in 2021. They also predicts stock price movement, but with a much higher accuracy, 70% to 80%.

Po said that the research paper from University of Illinois at Chicago violates the meaning of “prediction”. He explained “prediction” means you train a model with data from a timeframe earlier the short squeeze happened. “However, that research’s training dataset and testing dataset are all come from the same timeframe, that is during the GME short squeeze incident. Therefore, the high accuracy from that research paper does not mean a lot.” he explained.
